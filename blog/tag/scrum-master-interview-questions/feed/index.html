Scrum Master Interview Questions And Answers

  1. What is Scrum?
    Ans. Scrum is an agile framework for managing and developing projects. It provides a flexible, collaborative approach to software development that emphasizes iterative progress, adaptability, and customer satisfaction. Scrum organizes work into time-boxed iterations called sprints, typically lasting two to four weeks.
  2. Define the roles in Scrum?

Ans.

  • Product Owner: Represents the stakeholders, defines the product backlog, and ensures the team is working on the most valuable features.
  • Scrum Master: Facilitates the Scrum process, removes impediments, and ensures the team follows the Scrum principles.
  • Development Team: A cross-functional group responsible for delivering the product incrementally.

3.What are the responsibilities of the Scrum Team?

Ans.
The Scrum Team collaboratively works on delivering a potentially releasable product increment each sprint. This includes planning, designing, developing, testing, and delivering the product.

4.Differentiate Between Agile and Scrum.

Ans.

  • Agile: A broad set of principles emphasizing adaptability, collaboration, and customer feedback in software development.
  • Scrum: A specific agile framework with defined roles, events, and artifacts for managing work in iterative cycles.

5.What are the Artifacts of the Scrum Process?

Ans.

  • Product Backlog: An ordered list of features and enhancements representing the work to be done.
  • Sprint Backlog: The subset of the product backlog selected for a sprint.
  • Increment: The sum of all the completed product backlog items in a sprint.

6.How are the Product and Sprint Backlog different from One Another?

Ans.

  • Product Backlog: Contains all features, enhancements, and fixes for a product.
  • Sprint Backlog: Contains the subset of items from the product backlog that the team commits to completing during a specific sprint.

7.Who is a Scrum Master? And what does he/she do?

Ans.

  • Scrum Master: A facilitator and servant-leader for the Scrum Team.
  • Responsibilities: Removes impediments, coaches the team in Scrum principles, and ensures adherence to Scrum practices.

8.What happens in Daily Stand-up sessions?

Ans.

  • A brief, daily meeting where the Scrum Team discusses progress, challenges, and plans for the day.
  • Each team member answers three questions: What did I do yesterday? What will I do today? Are there any impediments?

9.What is Scrum-ban?

Ans.

  • A hybrid approach that combines Scrum and Kanban principles.
  • Allows for flexibility by incorporating elements of both frameworks.

10.What is Sprint 0 and Spike?

Ans.

  • Sprint 0: An optional initial sprint for project setup, planning, and preparation.
  • Spike: A time-boxed investigation or research task undertaken to gather information or reduce uncertainty about a specific aspect of the project.
