In fact, the opposite is typically true.

Globs, or “glob patterns,” are simpler and have a more limited syntax compared to regular expressions. Regular expressions (regex) are a powerful and flexible pattern-matching language that allows for complex pattern-matching and text manipulation. They can be quite extensive and have a steeper learning curve.

Glob patterns, on the other hand, are a simpler form of pattern matching that often use wildcard characters like * and ? to match files or strings based on simple patterns. Glob patterns are easier to understand and use for basic matching needs, but they are less expressive and versatile compared to regular expressions.
