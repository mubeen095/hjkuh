Infosys Exam Syllabus -FAQs

Q1.What is the Infosys exam syllabus?

Ans. The Infosys exam syllabus covers topics on mathematical ability, verbal ability, and technical skills.

Q2.Are there any specific technical skills covered in the exam?

Ans. The technical skills section may cover topics related to Programming languages (such as Java, Python, and C++), Database Management Systems (DBMS), Data Structures and Algorithms, Operating Systems Computer Networks, and Object-Oriented Programming Concepts.

Q3.How can I prepare for the Infosys exam?

Ans. To prepare for the Infosys exam, you can: understand the exam pattern and syllabus thoroughly. Practice regularly with sample papers and previous year’s question papers. Focus on weak areas and practice more in those topics.

Q4.Is there any negative marking in the Infosys exam?

Ans. No, there is no negative marking in the Infosys exam. Candidates are encouraged to attempt all questions.

Q5.How can I get the detailed syllabus for the Infosys exam?

Q6.Can I expect questions from previous exams in the Infosys exam?

Ans. While the exact questions may not repeat, practicing questions from previous exams can give you an idea about the pattern and difficulty level of the exam.

Q7.What are the topics of the Mathematical Ability section?

Ans. The topics in the mathematical ability section are Ratio and Proportion, Profit and Loss, Interest (Simple and Compound), and Time and Work.

Q8. What are the topics of Pseudo Code?

Ans. The topics in Pseudo Code are Python and C++, Algorithms, and Data Structures.
